A SOCIAL NETWORK THAT LET'S YOU POP THE BUBBLE

Bubble is a social network where users pop bubble links that have not been selectively generated by algorithms. These bubble links are loosely categorised by photos, news, memes, videos and user created posts.

DISCOVER FREELY

Social media networks are computer-meditated technologies that allow users to interact, share, create and express information through virtual communities and networks. This electronic communication can take away from our day to day decision making where we find ourselves reading a headline, tapping and scrolling endlessly under a controlling algorithm. With social networks like Facebook, an algorithm feeds up data we are most likely to engage with.

Allows for greater expressions

Bubble seeks to give the user an unbiased way to engage and find content relevant to a core set of structured interests. To create a social network that doesn’t selectively guess what the user gets to see is the goal – the network will show information that the users may disagree with, forcing out echo chambers, allowing for all viewpoints and expressions to be freely explored.

(Technology such as social media) “lets you go off with like-minded people, so you're not mixing and sharing and understanding other points of view ... It's super important. It's turned out to be more of a problem than I, or many others, would have expected.” — Bill Gates 2017
